The problem

Are you losing money on jobs without knowing it?

01

QuickBooks, Xero, FreshBooks — they handle general accounting fine. But they don't know what a CSI cost code is, can't track job profitability across change orders, and weren't built for how electricians, plumbers, or GCs actually invoice and estimate work. You end up with a shoebox of receipts and a spreadsheet that's always 3 weeks behind.

02

By the time your bookkeeper catches a coding mistake, the profit margin on that job is already gone. You underbid the next project because you don't know what the last one actually cost. For small construction companies, that's the difference between growing and going under.

03

Construction ERP systems like Procore or Sage are built for 50-person operations. If you're a plumbing contractor with 3 crews or an electrician running 10 jobs, you're stuck between spreadsheets and software that costs more than your office rent.

How it works

Upload your invoices. JobLedger handles the job costing. QuickBooks gets the right entries.

Step 1

Upload your invoices, estimates, and timesheets

Drop in PDFs, photos of receipts, or forward emails — even handwritten notes, blurry photos, and coffee-stained invoices. JobLedger extracts vendor, amount, date, line items, and job references from whatever format your subs and suppliers send you.

Step 2

AI maps every cost to the right job and category

JobLedger learns your job numbering and maps each invoice to the correct project and cost code — whether you use CSI divisions, custom codes, or your own system. Ambiguous items get flagged. Nothing is silently misclassified.

Step 3

Entries post straight to QuickBooks

Review and approve, then entries post directly to your QuickBooks. Job profitability stays accurate, cash flow projections are reliable, and you stop spending evenings on data entry.
